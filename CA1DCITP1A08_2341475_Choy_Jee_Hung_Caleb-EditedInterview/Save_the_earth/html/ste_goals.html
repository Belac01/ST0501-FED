Goals and objectives

In order to achieve a sustainable future, we must all do our part in cutting down on waste and our global carbon footprint. We have to make decisions based on long-term consequences instead of always taking the cheapest and easiest solution to our fulfil our needs. There are 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) to end poverty, save the earth and try to improve the standards of living for people worldwide, rich or poor.

Goal 1: Reducing hunger worldwide

Hunger and malnutrition is a barrier to a sustainable future. It causes people to be more prone to diseases and become less productive thus making them not able to have a proper income and livelihood. A world with lesser hunger issues can improve our economies, health, education, equality and our social development.

Some examples that we can follow:

  • Support local markets and make sustainable food choices
  • Have a good diet that is nutritious and healthy
  • Fight food waste by not wasting food unneccsarily

Goal 2: Using only clean and affordable energy

For a long time, our main sources of energy has been through the burning of coal, oil and gas. But did you know that it contributes to a large amount of carbon dioxide? This not only affects the environment through climate change, but would also affect the health and well-being of current and future generations through air pollution. Therefore, the solution would be to use clean and renewable energy sources such as solar energy, and cutting down on unneccsary usage of energy.

Some examples that we can follow:

  • Turning off devices when not in use
  • Using energy effcient devices that cuts down on the usage of electricity
  • Investing and switching to more renewable sources of energy such as solar energy or wind energy

Goal 3: Producing and consuming responsibly

Economic and social progress that we have made has been followed by environmental problems that could very well be endangering our future development and survival. There are many aspects that needs to be changed, ranging from food and product wastage to polltution of the land, water and air that we rely on for survival.

Some examples that we can follow:

  • Reducing your personal waste
  • Be more aware of what you buy and try to choose the sustainable options
  • Reduce, Reuse and Recycle more

We do not inherit the Earth from our ancestors; we borrow it from our children. Be part of the solution now.
