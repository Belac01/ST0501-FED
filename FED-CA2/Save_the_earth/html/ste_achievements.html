SP Environmental Policy

Singapore Polytechnic is commited to caring for the environement by going green and educating the importance of environmental sustainability. SP is commited to ensuring that pollution is controled through the 3Rs, Reduce Reuse Recycle, and has always complied with the goverment's environmental legislation and requirments.

Singapore's first ever solar photovoltaics recycling line

The recycling line takes apart solar panels, removing the important parts such as materials like silver and glass to be recycled for other uses. It boasts a recycling capacity of 80 panels a day and that about 95% of the solar panel can be reobtained.

SP has also set up 2 labs, SP-Sembcorp Solar PV Research Lab and SP-CHINT Smart Electrical Power Training Laboratory, that would greatly increase the amount of research and development done on solar energy.

Collaboration with Sembcorp and Chint

SP has teamed up with Sembcorp and Sembcorp and Chint to innovate sustainable energy solutions and build a talent pipeline for the green energy and photovoltaics industry in Asia. They anounced initiatives to hasten the adoptation of renewable and smart energy in Singapore such as launching Singapore's first every solar photovoltaics recycling line, deepening Singapore's solar and smart energy knowledge, positioning Singapore as a regional hub for Sustainable energy solutions.

The creation and usage of the ESEEC

SP has established a new ESEEC, to work with SMEs. The aim of this centre is to help with carbon management through the sharing of knowledge and resources. The centre acts as a all in one hub that helps companies to build a strong digital capabilities thorugh a range of approaches for the mindsets, skillsets and behaviors.

There are initiatives that help to provide real world learning such as indsutry attachements, consultancy projects, and the provision of intensive leadership and senior management programme for businesses that are growing or have the potential to grow. All of which would help with the improvement and change SMEs for the better.

Recycling plastics into asphalt

A local construction company, Samwoh, has partnered with SP to utilise plastic waste in asphalt, which is material used to build more durable roads surfaces. Through the recycling of plastic waste, the plastic pollution in the environment can be reduced.

Through this partnership and collaboration, it has been found that asphalt made with plastic waste could last at least 20 percent longer than regular asphalt and it had levels of chemicals used that were within the offical guidelines. This is a huge step in the recycling industry as plastic is not easily recycled due to its undecoposing state.
